Eco-Friendly Way to celebrate HOLI and Science behind it...

In. Indian culture there are many festivals to celebrate and enjoy.
But, in ancinet times thes festivals were celebrated very scientifically,
without harming the nature. We celebrate the same festivals in the same way...

But we have forgotten the science behind it use din the ancient times and celebrate it blindly,
while polluting the nature...

This is one of our favorite festival but nowadays HOLI is not a festival
it has become something more dangerous but we can prevent this by following ways.


  • 1.Celebrate it in a dry way
    India wastes as much water as the whole United Kingdom consumes in a year.
    We must reserve our water before a serious worst water crisis hits India.
    If you are playing wet Holi, there is often the use of pichkaris and water balloons will create unnecessary garbage,
    waste a lot of water and pollute the environment. Therefore, celebrate a dry Holi to avoid such unnecessary harm to the environment.
    So, this Holi is a responsible citizen and celebrate dry Holi.

  • 2.Don’t force someone to play Holy or throw colors at random people
    Dont force someone to play holi with you if they are worried about thier
    skin, hair or coronavirus do not force them to play as it is their choice.
    For who knows that the color you are using is a very hazardrous colors,
    and could be a cause of many skin dirsorders and complications...
  • 3.Avoid playing in mud and dirty places
    Many people go without thinking the dankgers and start to play in the wet mud.
    They sit and lie down in dirty places without realizing the risks of getting sick.
    Celebrating Holi in the middle of coronavirus outbreak can be uncertain,
    so avoid playing in the mud or dirty places. This could also lead to some problems in the body,
    as we would not notice when we accidently swallowed the mud which coudl either lead to,
    stomach upsets or in thw worst case respitory problems.
  • 4. Play Holi with natural colors
    Playing Holi with natural colors is the best way to celebrate this colorful festival.
    Don’t ruin it by using harmful and irrelevant products like petrol, oil paints, mud, and other chemical-based products.
    These products are not only hazardous to your health but also to the environment.
    It also harms the skins and leaves undesired effect on skin and is hard to clean.
    The natural colors are give the same joy and are eco-friendly, they decompose quickly,
    and are easily washed away even.
  • 5.Avoid the use of balloons & plastic bags
    Water balloons are made of rubber and plastic, while Pichkaris are simply made using plastic alone.
    Our country landfills are already burdened with such overflowing garbage.
    These things don't even decompose in a thousand years... Thus, celbrating HOLI in this way,

  • 6.Decorate your house with recycled or paper products
    We shouldn't use plastic decorations during Holi and choose an environmentally friendly way to decorate our home.
    Most of the festive decorations nowadays are mostly made from plastic which can cause harm to the environment.
    This can easily be achieved bu using waster paper and newspaper, the way keeping our decorations
    neat, beautiful and ina eco-friendly manner.
  • 7. Eco-friendly Holi bonfire
    The burning of fuelwood to create the bonfire presents another serious environmental problem.
    Considering that approximately 30,000 bonfires are lit in the state of Gujarat alone just for one season,
    this leads to wastage of a staggering amount of wood.Instead of using precious wood by chopping off the trees,
    one can burn eco-friendly waste for the Holi bonfire which will not create any air pollution.
    Use ingredients like cow-dung cakes, coconut waste, and camphor to light up.
  • 8.Prepare homemade sweets and avoid outside food
    To avoid any kind of contamination during this coronavirus outbreak,
    prepare all of your food and sweets at home.
    Instead of eating outside and buying sweets from shops, you can prepare delicious sweets and dishes
    by following cooking videos and apps.
  • 9.Avoid eating in styrofoam cups and bowls
    We see a lot of styrofoam plates, plastic cutlery and plastic cups lying around.
    Even during the celebration of Holi, the scene is not much different.
    Free foods are served in every corner and people from all sections are welcome to eat,
    but with this rises the problem of non-biodegradable trash. So we should use eco-friendly disposables
    as they easily decompose without harming the environment...
